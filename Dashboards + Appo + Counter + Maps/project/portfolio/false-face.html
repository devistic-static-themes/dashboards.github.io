CASE STUDY

False Face

THE CLIENT

False Face is a global electronic music record label based in Canada. False Face collaborates with a roster of artists and events in conjunction with their record releases on the digital music stores. This includes sales on Spotify, Apple, Amazon, Beatport, and other digital music stores. I was approached before the launch, to create the concept for the brand. I was also responsible for art direction for album cover art and marketing content for the brand.

The Challenge

_ Design an exceptional brand identity for False Face Music to generate digital sales, - engagement, and event activation through digital media and advertising.
_ Design monthly album cover art to match the brand identity with artist representation.
_ Design promotional content for each release including image and video.
_ Design a portfolio website to host music releases, news, and event-related content.

The Outcome

Strong brand identity in the electronic music industry, local and international venues, and online music stores.

Logo

For the logo, I wanted to use the Idea of the mask and the two personalities in the story. So creating the feeling of two opposite sides of a visual element was important to me. I started drafting ideas by using the letter F as my main element for the logo. By flipping the letter, I was able to achieve the association with the brand visually and project the message hidden behind it. From that point, I started exploring colors that would complement my main brand colors and then created the typography and the style sheet for the brand.

Research
Details

During my research and discovery phase with the client, I sought to understand the current state of the music industry in general. My intention for this research was to uncover the needs of music listeners and figure out the best way to stand out visually and contextually from the saturated music market. Also to challenge piracy with fully branded material that is easy to identify for the fans.

Goals

1 - Define a music listener’s common behaviors when shopping on digital music stores.
2 - Define a music listener’s common behaviours when shopping online.
3 - Determine what music buyers and listeners approve or dislike about both streaming and purchasing music online.
4 - Define an essential target audience that is most interested in the genre, quality and purchasing music online.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed some of False Face’s competitors in the electronic music industry. I uncovered the strengths and weaknesses of False Face’s direct competitors Drumcode, Suara, and Bedrock Records.

My competitive analysis and discovery produced the following insights:

Access: Most users want instant access to streaming their favorite music on any device. They turn to social media, Youtube, and Spotify as their main source of listening and interacting with music. Music buyers also spend hours browsing and listening to song previews before making a purchase.

Community: Most music buyers and streamers are influenced by social media and their favorite DJs or influencers. Most purchase music after listening to a sample on social media, or through watching a promo video. The brand needs to have a strong visual social presence.

Artist Communities: A big portion of electronic music purchases are by other DJ’s and artists. A music label needs to create promos and newsletters before each release to increase exposure.

Song recognition with Apps: Most music listeners and buyers use mobile applications like Shazam to identify songs at events and music festivals. Having a strong brand presence on music recognitions applications can generate plenty of sales and engagement.

Persona and Storyboard Creation

The research data and analytics lead to the creation of many personas to get more familiar with the brand target audience. Meet Kelly an upcoming DJ from Toronto. Passionate about music and performance Kelly’s personality trades impact her digital interaction with music applications and community.

After understanding the target audience and recognizing their requirements, I had to determine the answer. I considered the company goals, user goals, and technical requirements to find a happy medium for everyone involved. Once I had identified common objectives, I could move forward to building a concept and prototype.

Visual Design & Branding

False Face’s name was inspired by the idea that everyone in our society is putting on a mask to protect their true feelings and identity. And music is the only element that unmasks the listener. So I wanted this idea to be reflected in the design and the feel of the brand. I also wanted to make sure that the brand feels luxurious and more exclusive to the listener. To me, this would create a special bond with listeners, the artists, and fans of the label.

I envisioned darker colors for the backgrounds, complemented with sharp and shiny elements, perhaps a gold or silver color. My vision was to create a nostalgic feeling for the fans, that represented opening a luxury gift box. I kept thinking about a satin feeling for the surface of the backgrounds and covers, which led to me create dark and slightly noisy backgrounds.

Merchandise Design

I implemented the concept into apparel and merchandise design for False Face. The merchandise encouraged fans to get more engaged with the label. It gives them a chance to own their favorite album cover as a wearable product. This was another channel to promote brand awareness and generate additional revenue.

Website

Although the style and format of the website evolved, during my work with the client, the main purpose and goal were to always keep the content simple and easy to access for users. The website was meant to serve as a portfolio and a hub for hosting all the music releases and information about artists. It was also a platform to demonstrate the artwork and the artists involved with the brand.

Cover Art Animation

Inspired by the album covers, I developed a style for the promotional 30 Second music videos to follow each release. Since most of the cover art was hand crafted, it made it easier to separately film each element and manipulate them in Adobe After Effects.

Music Video, Stop Motion Animation

In 2018 I art directed and produced a stop motion, hand crafted, music video for one of the releases on False Face by Kinza (Artist). The song was called Cupid’s Arrow and had a very animated feel to it. Taking inspiration from the title, I created a number of sequences with stop motion photography and animation. I used coloured paper and a few other props from my studio, to build a story for this music video.
