CASE STUDY

bContent

THE CLIENT

bContent is a user-based content marketing platform that allows customers to signup and tries it for free. Users browse through a variety of content writing and lead generation services and make purchases through the platform. Clients can use the internal messaging app on the platform to communicate with the bContent team. They can view order status and make comments on their orders. With notifications, users are alerted every time any activity appears on their account related to orders or messages.

The Brand

I was approached to build a user-based platform to centralize my client’s various content marketing services and websites. To centralize all services, I first had to understand the scope of my client’s service offerings. I was able to finalize a list of 40 services that would be centralized through the platform. The first step was creating a brand name. After my research on SEO, related keywords, and industry competitors, I provided ten possible options for the name to my client. I had my mind sent on bContent and I was happy when the client had the same final choice.

Research
Details

During my research and discovery with the client, I sought to understand the current challenges of content marketing and all related branches. My intention for this research was to uncover content marketing challenges for medium size and enterprise businesses. I wanted to find out what elements can set apart my envisioned platform from other competitors. The goal was to build an environment that allows users to make quick orders as well as contacting a live strategist at anytime for more assistance.

Goals

1 - Define business challenges by producing branded content and using content marketing platforms.
2 - Define a user’s common behaviours when working with an online content marketing platform.
3 - Determine what business owners and marketers approve or dislike about digital content marketing platforms.
4 - Define an essential target audience that is most interested in launching content marketing campaigns.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed some of the competitors in content marketing. I uncovered the strengths and weaknesses of bContent’s direct competitors Percolate, Mintent, and Buzzsumo.

My competitive analysis and discovery produced the following insights:

Access: Most business owners and content marketers want instant access to centralized content writing and marketing platform. They turn to online digital platforms, Social Media Groups, and YouTube as their main source of news to find industry-related tools and platforms.

Quality & Process: It is highly important to users of the platform to be able to receive quality content for the campaigns. Delivery timelines, customer support, and secure payments were amongst the most important factors in working with an online platform.

Human Touch: Most business owners and marketers are hesitant to commit to new platforms if they are not able to connect with a human during their first experience with the application.

Measuring Results: Tracking and reporting on order and campaign status also play an important part user’s decision making. If they are paying for services, they expect notifications, followup and project status to be available to them at all times.

Persona and Storyboard Creation

The research data and analytics lead to the creation of many personas to understand the target audience. Meet Juliet a content marketing specialist from Montreal. Committed to her role at the marketing agency, she is determined to find the best content marketing platform for the company.

After understanding the target audience and recognizing their requirements, I had to determine the answer. I considered the company goals, user goals, and technical requirements to find a happy medium for everyone involved. Once I had identified common objectives, I could move forward to building a concept and prototype.
