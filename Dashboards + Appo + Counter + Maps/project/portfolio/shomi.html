CASE STUDY

shomi!

THE CLIENT

shomi! Inc. is an experiential and print company based in Canada. They specialize in creating fabric display banners, animated lightboxes, and billboard displays for some of the top brands in North America. In 2017 I was approached to rebuild shomi’s existing website that had not been maintained for over ten five years. Their goal with redesigning the website was to create brand awareness, unify the brand identity, build an e-commerce platform for their products, and Provide downloadable instructions and sales documents.

Deliverables

● Art Direction
● UX/UI Design
● Brand Redesign
● Content Writing
● Website Re-Design
● Landing Pages
● Visual Design

The Challenge

_ Re-design and develop a new website for shomi
_ Unify company brand identity across all channels
_ Optimize the website content for search engines and keywords
_Make all product documentation downloadable through the website
_ Design an impactful social media strategy

The Outcome

A newly designed website with a strong brand identity, e-commerce options, and product documentation.

Role

Project Director / User Experience Design / Web Development / Branding / Art Direction / Client Success Mapping / Print Design

Branding

My client wanted to stick with their original logo design, so my goal was to give the brand a fresh look but still stay true to the original logo.

I decided to keep the original coloring of the brand and only create a few additional options for building the website and marketing collateral. I also animated their existing logo to be used on explainer videos and other visual components of the brand.

Research
Details

I enjoyed working with my client on this project because it was something new for me. The challenge of being involved with a brand that delivers quality work to such a niche market was appealing and exciting for me. During my research and discovery phase with the client, I had to understand the experiential market and how businesses interact with their service providers such as my client. My goal for my research was to look at business to business communication in the industry, client communication, and internal workflow. I also had to look at how shomi currently operates with existing clients in terms of managing their projects internally and how the orders are fulfilled and delivered.

Goals

1 - Define the experiential market’s B2B communication and online ordering behaviors.
2 - Define a business’s common behaviours looking for print services online.
3 - Determine what businesses approve or dislike about the B2B experiential marketing communications.
4 - Define an essential target audience that is most interested in shomi’s product offerings and services.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed some of shomi’s competitors in the tension fabric display industry. I uncovered the strengths and weaknesses of shomi’s direct competitors PNH, 40 Visuals, and SEG Systems.

My competitive analysis and discovery produced the following insights:

Customer Service: The most important element and the deciding factor for most clients in the experiential marketing industry, is customer service. Delivering quick custom quotes and estimates plus followups and engagement with the service provider were pointed out by a number of B2B clients.

Quotes & Estimates: Since experiential and printed fabric displays are mostly customized products, it is highly important to provide fast quotation and order estimates for the clients.

Post Production & Installation: Once a purchase is made and the product is shipped to clients, they always refer to their hardware provider for additional support such as installation documentation and instructions.

Documentation and Downloads: The ability to view and download instructions for product assembly and packaging is highly important to clients. Also for the resellers and affiliates accessing the sales documents quickly can improve their performance.

Persona and Storyboard Creation

The research data and analytics lead to the creation of many personas to get more familiar with the brand target audience. Meet Andrew an auto repair shop owner in Toronto. Passionate about his work and his business, Andrew strongly believes that a experiential marketing and display can greatly impact his shop traffic. He is looking to partner up with a company that can centralize his print marketing and trade show needs.

After understanding the target audience and recognizing their requirements, I had to determine the answer. I considered the company goals, user goals, and technical requirements to find a happy medium for everyone involved. Once I had identified common objectives, I could move forward to building a concept and prototype.

Technical Goals:

- E-commerce Web Platform with Product Catalogue
- On-Site Chat Bot
- Downloadable Content

Product Illustrations

After reviewing all of shomi’s products, I realize that one of the biggest challenges for them is to demonstrate the differences between each product. For that reason handcrafted illustrations for each product category, that are instantly recognizable and distinguished.

Team Illustrations

In order to improve the brand identity and build a bond a closer between the customers and the company, I created illustrations for each of the team members and used them on the About section of the website.

Branding & Website Design

I finally had a concept and I was ready to move forward with the full branding and website redesign. I also managed to revamp the company handbook, sales documentation, and business cards in the process. One of my main goals was to make sure anything related to the brand has unified messaging and theme across all channels.

Explainer Video & Illustrated Animation

Based on the company handbook and the sales documents I had already created, I designed a few explainer videos and sales promo videos to demonstrate products, build company culture, and build brand awareness.

With the new website and product pages ready to go, it was time for me to add copy and content, then optimize the landing pages to gain ranking on the search engines and bring more traffic to the website. This was an ongoing process that took over a year to complete. However, it was important to build impactful landing pages and make sure that they are directed to a very specific target audience about a very specific product. The results were three new product landing pages that generated new traffic on the website and ranked on the top page of search for their related keywords within 6 months.
