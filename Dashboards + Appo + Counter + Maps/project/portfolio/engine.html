Here is a basic checklist to go through when you start your Search engine optimization:

Research your keywords. Research the keywords your customers would use to find you on search engines. Try using keyword tools that provide insight into the strength of each keyword. KWFinder is one of my favorite tools to use. Check them out at https://kwfinder.com/.

Make sure that all your links are pointing to relevant (and working) URLs and all internal links on your website are correctly connected.

Page titles and Meta Descriptions on your page must always meet search engine standards. Your Page title is what displays on search engine result pages as your heading. It is the clickable headline of your page. Your titles should always be an accurate representation of the content on your website and 10 to 60 characters in length.

Meta descriptions appear directly below your title and motivate users to click. Think of your meta descriptions as advertising copy, sufficiently descriptive and between 50 to 300 characters.

URL‚Äôs for each page on your website must not exceed 75 characters in length. Try avoiding long URL links and choose a human-readable and natural language. Placing the appropriate keywords in your URL also increases your SEO ranking.

Avoid duplicate titles and duplicate content on your pages. Your title tags should have similar content such as your keywords, but make sure they indicate what is different to that page. Consider who your primary searcher is and how they might look for your page, then build your titles around those behaviors.

Name your images correctly. Remember that image names and descriptions play a role in your keyword and SEO ranking. Use your keywords in your image file name. Be sure to describe your images as much as possible in the caption.
